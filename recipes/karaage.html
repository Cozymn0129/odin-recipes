Karaage

Description

Karaage is a beloved Japanese dish consisting of bite-sized pieces of chicken that are marinated, coated in starch, and deep-fried to golden perfection. Unlike Western fried chicken, karaage often uses potato starch (or a mix with flour), which creates a thinner, crispier coating that clings tightly to the juicy meat inside. The marinade—usually a mix of soy sauce, sake, mirin, ginger, and garlic—infuses the chicken with savoury, slightly sweet, and aromatic flavours before it even hits the oil. The result is chicken that’s crispy on the outside, succulent on the inside, and packed with umami.
Karaage is incredibly versatile and can be enjoyed in many ways. It’s often served simply with a wedge of lemon to brighten the flavour, or paired with shredded cabbage, rice, or miso soup as part of a set meal. In izakaya (Japanese pubs), karaage is a popular snack to accompany beer, while in bento boxes it’s a go-to protein because it stays tasty even when cold. Beyond chicken, the karaage technique can be applied to fish, squid, or even tofu, making it more of a style of cooking than just a single dish. Its balance of crispiness, juiciness, and savoury depth has earned it a place as one of Japan’s most iconic comfort foods.

Ingredients

  • 500 g chicken thighs (skin-on, cut into bite-sized chunks)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, grated
  • 1/2 cup potato starch (or cornstarch)
  • Oil for deep-frying
  • Lemon wedges

Steps

  1. Mix soy sauce, sake, mirin, ginger, and garlic. Marinate chicken 30 mins (not too long or it gets salty).
  2. Heat oil to 170-180 °C.
  3. Dredge chicken lightly in potato starch, shake off excess.
  4. Fry in batches until golden brown and crisp, about 4-5 mins.
  5. Drain on paper towels, serve with lemon wedges.
