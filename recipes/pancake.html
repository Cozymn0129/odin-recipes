Pancake

Description

A pancake is a simple yet universally loved dish made from a batter of flour, eggs, milk, and a leavening agent such as baking powder, cooked on a hot griddle or frying pan. Its charm lies in its versatility—thin and delicate like crêpes in France, thick and fluffy in the American style, or somewhere in between depending on culture and recipe. The surface usually turns golden brown while the inside stays soft and tender, making it the perfect base for sweet or savoury toppings.
Across the world, pancakes take on many delicious identities. In the UK, they are traditionally thin and often enjoyed with a squeeze of lemon and sugar on Shrove Tuesday. In Japan, “hotcakes” are extra thick and airy, sometimes stacked high and served almost like a dessert. Meanwhile, in the United States and Canada, pancakes are a breakfast classic, often drenched in butter and maple syrup. From potato latkes to Korean pajeon, countless variations exist, but they all share the same comforting essence: simple ingredients transformed into something warm, satisfying, and endlessly adaptable.

Ingredients

  • 200 g plain flour
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 250 ml milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp melted butter (plus extra for cooking)
  • Maple syrup & butter

Steps

  1. Mix flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl.
  2. In another bowl, whisk milk, egg, and melted butter.
  3. Combine wet and dry ingredients until just mixed (lumps are fine).
  4. Heat a non-stick pan with a little butter. Pour in ¼ cup batter per pancake.
  5. Cook until bubbles form on top, flip, and cook until golden.
  6. Stack high, serve with butter and maple syrup.
