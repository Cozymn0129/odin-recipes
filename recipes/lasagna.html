Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made by layering wide, flat sheets of pasta with rich fillings such as ragù (a slow-cooked meat and tomato sauce), béchamel, and cheese, most famously mozzarella or Parmigiano-Reggiano. Baked until bubbling and golden, it combines soft pasta, creamy sauce, and savoury depth in every bite. Its structure makes it both hearty and comforting, often served as a centrepiece at family gatherings or festive occasions. While traditional recipes emphasise simplicity and balance, lasagna’s layered format leaves endless room for variation.
Outside Italy, lasagna has been reinterpreted in many creative ways. Vegetarian versions swap the meat for spinach, mushrooms, or roasted vegetables, while some modern adaptations use seafood, pesto, or even replace the pasta with alternatives like aubergine slices or zucchini. In the United States, lasagna has become a beloved household staple, often loaded with extra cheese and baked in large pans for sharing. This adaptability, paired with its indulgent, satisfying character, has helped lasagna cement its place as one of the world’s favourite comfort foods.

Ingredients

  • 12 sheets of lasagna pasta
  • 500 g ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 800 g canned tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • Olive oil, salt, pepper, oregano, basil
  • 500 ml béchamel sauce (butter, flour, milk, nutmeg)
  • 200 g mozzarella
  • 100 g Parmesan

Steps

  1. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil, add meat, brown well.
  2. Stir in tomato paste and canned tomatoes, season with herbs, salt, and pepper. Simmer 30-40 minutes until thick.
  3. Prepare béchamel: melt butter, whisk in flour, slowly add milk, stir until smooth. Season with nutmeg, salt, pepper.
  4. In a baking dish, layer: pasta → meat sauce → béchamel → mozzarella → repeat.
  5. Finish with béchamel and plenty of Parmesan.
  6. Bake at 180 °C for 40 minutes, rest 10 minutes before serving.
